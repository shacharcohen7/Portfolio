The Very Beginning

In high school, I wasn’t sure what truly interested me, but almost by chance I chose computer science. At first I didn’t fully understand what I was doing, but at some point everything started to click. I discovered the beauty of writing code and the satisfaction of making something work. My passion grew during the QA course in the IDF, where I felt confident in the programming lessons and was fascinated by automation. Seeing how every element has its own place and behavior excited me, and I started to appreciate the meticulous organization behind web development.

First Experience in High-Tech

My experience as a QA engineer in the army helped me transition smoothly into the civilian workforce at Biosense Webster. There, I had the incredible opportunity to be part of a real-world software team. I learned how to collaborate effectively within my team and across different teams, working closely with other QA engineers, developers, and system engineers. I discovered that I was not only good at my job but also at building strong, professional, and friendly relationships. Even during my studies, I kept in touch with the team and returned during summer breaks to continue contributing.

Ben Gurion University

And then came the main event—pursuing my BSc in Software Engineering at Ben-Gurion University. The studies were challenging, but they pushed my logical thinking to new heights. Along the way, I had the opportunity to work on practical projects that allowed me to gain valuable experience in teamwork and experiment with various programming languages. I’m incredibly proud of how far I’ve come, and now, in my final semester with only three courses left, I’m excited and fully prepared to take the next step in my career.

My Calling - Frontend Developer

Throughout my academic journey, I found myself consistently drawn to frontend development. There’s something incredibly rewarding about visually representing data on a website and designing an intuitive, aesthetically pleasing user interface. I have a strong sense of design and a deep appreciation for visual appeal. Combined with the technical knowledge I’ve gained, I believe my true calling is to be a frontend developer, where I can bring ideas to life and create seamless digital experiences.
