Introduction

The Very Beginning

In high school I wasn’t sure what truly interested me, but almost by chance I chose the field of computer science. At first I didn’t fully understand what I was doing, but at some point everything started to click. I discovered the beauty of writing code and the satisfaction of making something work. My passion grew during the QA course, where I enjoyed the java programming lessons and was fascinated by automation. Seeing how every element has its own place and behavior excited me, and I started to appreciate the meticulous organization behind web development.

First Experience in High-Tech

My experience as a QA engineer in the army helped me transition smoothly into the civilian workforce at Biosense Webster. There, I had the opportunity to be part of a real-world software team. I learned how to collaborate effectively within my team and across different teams, working closely with developers and system engineers. I discovered that I was not only good at my job but also at building strong, professional, and friendly relationships. During my studies, I kept in touch with the team and returned during summer breaks to continue contributing.

Ben Gurion University

And then came the main event - pursuing my BSc in Software Engineering at Ben-Gurion University. The studies were challenging, but they pushed my logical thinking to new heights. Along the way, I had the opportunity to work on practical projects that allowed me to gain valuable experience in teamwork and experiment with various programming languages. I am really proud of how far I’ve come, and now I am excited to put my knowledge into practice and take the next step in my career.

My Calling - Frontend Developer

Throughout my academic journey, I found myself consistently drawn to frontend development. There’s something incredibly rewarding about visually representing data on a website and designing an intuitive, aesthetically pleasing user interface. I have a strong sense of design and a deep appreciation for visual appeal. Combined with the technical knowledge I’ve gained, I believe my true calling is to be a frontend developer, where I can bring ideas to life and create seamless digital experiences.
